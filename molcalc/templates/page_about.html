What is MolCalc@ASU?

MolCalc@ASU is based on MolCalc, a web-based chemistry teaching tool built originally by the Jensen Group at the University of Copenhagen and being extended by the Yarger Research Group in the School of Molecular Sciences at Arizona State University that allows chemists to build small molecules and estimate key molecular properties using research-grade quantum chemistry software. Currently, MolCalc@ASU can provide, in a matter of minutes or seconds, a decent sense of the physical and chemical properties of a chosen molecule, such as

  • energy-minimized molecular structure
  • electronic structure and molecular orbitals
  • thermochemical properties of the molecular gas form
  • vibrational modes and their frequencies

Why do we need MolCalc@ASU?

MolCalc was originally designed for teaching as opposed to research and, therefore, places an emphasis on running computations quickly. Indeed, so long as qualitative accuracy is sufficient, the speed of calculations if of primary importance since humans learn most effectively when real-time feedback is available. MolCalc@ASU is being developed in this spirit and is currently being stress-tested in the context of the upper-division CHM 343 lab course at ASU in Fall 2022. However, the ASU MolCalc project has longer-term ambitions driven by the central goal to improve student engagement with fundamental concepts in chemistry and physics.

Emphasis on physical intuition

The broader ASU MolCalc effort is focused on leveraging computational methods to deeply integrate physical intuition with real or simulated experimental data using clickable explanations, dynamic visualizations, and interactive data. A guiding focus of our development efforts is to make meaning transparent using a web interface that provides explanations in a visual context, allowing the user to concentrate on concepts rather than deciphering complicated programs and arcane terminologies.

Cloud-based physics and chemistry

Like the original MolCalc project, ASU MolCalc will continue being developed as an open source interactive chemistry teaching tool. ASU MolCalc serves as the starting point for the development of a more comprehensive visual-centric interface for cloud-based physics and chemistry lab courses. The idea is to have students develop a "chemical intuition" about how molecular structure affects molecular properties, without performing the underlying calculations by hand (which would be near impossible for all but the simplest chemical systems). In particular, we envision developing a tool that establishes concrete conceptual pathways from representative experimental plots and data to the mechanistic physical/chemical picture that directly underlies that data.

What technologies does MolCalc@ASU use?

MolCalc@ASU uses standard HTML and CSS3, along with the technologies and software packages described below.

Front-end components

  • JavaScript (jQuery)
  • JSmol

Back-end components

  • Python ((Pyramid)
  • SQLAlchemy
  • JavaScript (jQuery)
  • GAMESS
  • Orca (coming soon!)

How does MolCalc perform electronic structure calculations?

Great question! Currently, MolCalc currently uses The General Atomic and Molecular Electronic Structure System (GAMESS), which is maintained by the Gordon research group at Iowa State University. We are also in the process of incorporating Orca as a user-selectable option for quantum chemistry calculation.
